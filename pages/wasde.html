World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates (WASDE)

The latest WASDE report was released on January 12, 2021. Information contained in the report can be found below. Click a crop name below to jump to that crop.

Corn Soybeans Cotton Rice Wheat

Corn

January projections for U.S. corn production was adjusted down 324 million bushels on lower yields and slight reduction in acres. Corn yield was decreased from 175.8 bushels per acre to 172 bushels per acre. Corn exports were lowered 100 million bushels to reflect tighter supplies and increased expected price. Corn ending stocks were lowered to 1.552 billion bushels with an average farm price of $4.20.

Soybeans

Soybean production was adjusted down in the January report to 4.135 billion bushels. This reduction was led by lower production in Minnesota, Iowa, and Kansas. Yield is projected at 50.2 bushels an acre. Soybean crush projections were raised 5 million bushels and the export forecast is raised 30 million bushels. Soybean exports are projected at a record 2.23 billion bushels. Ending stocks are projected lower at 140 million bushels with an average farm price of $11.15/bushel.

Cotton

The January WASDE report saw another large reduction in cotton production by nearly a million bales. Production is projected at 14.95 million bales and yield at 825 pounds an acre. U.S. export projection were raised slightly to 15.25 million bales. Ending stocks were reduced to 4.60 million bales and average farm price is projected at 68 cents per pound.
