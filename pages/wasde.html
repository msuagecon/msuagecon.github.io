World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates (WASDE)

The latest WASDE report was released on December 10th, 2020. Information contained in the report can be found below. Click a crop name below to jump to that crop.

Corn Soybeans Cotton Rice Wheat

Corn

December U.S. corn supply and use projections for the 2020/21 marketing year remained unchanged from last month. Total U.S. corn production is projected at 14.5 billion bushels with 82.5 million harvested acres and 175.8 bushels per acre yield. The main story with corn going forward will continue to revolve around the record high export projection of 2.65 billion bushels. Ending stocks are projected at 1.702 billion bushels with an average farm price of $4.00 per bushel.

Soybeans

The only change in the December soybean U.S. supply and use projections is an increase in domestic crushing and price. Crushings were increased by 15 million bushels and average farm price was increased to $10.55 per bushel. With higher crushings and exports unchanged, U.S. soybean ending stocks are projected at 175 million bushels. If realized, ending stocks would be the lowest since 2013/14. Ending stocks have seen dramatic reductions since the record high of 909 million bushels just two years ago.

Cotton

The December WASDE report for cotton saw a 1.1. million bale reduction in production to 15.95 million bales. The majority of the cut to production was due to a 900,000 bale reduction in Texas. U.S. export projection are raised 400,000 bales to 15.0 million. Ending stocks are projected at 5.70 million bales and average farm price is projected at 65.0 cents/lb. Even with the production reduction, the stocks-to-use ratio for cotton is the second highest since 2007/08.

Total Rice

The December WASDE report lowered rice supplies slightly to 291.3 million hundredweights based on lower imports of medium and short grain. Both rough and milled exports are decreased 1 million hundred weight from last month. Ending stocks are projected at 50.8 million hundredweight and average farm price is projected at $13.10.
