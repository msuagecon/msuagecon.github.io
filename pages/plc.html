Price Loss Coverage (PLC) Updates

The Price Loss Coverage Program (PLC) payment rate is determined by a reference price and marketing average price for each crop. The reference price is set by the Farm Bill and the marketing year average price is determeined by USDA each year. The marketing year average price represents the average price producers recieved for a commodity within a marketng year. Below are final payment rates for the 2019/2020 marketing year and the expected payment rates for 2020/2021 marketing year. The Expected PLC Payment Rate is the higher of (Reference Price minus the Estimated 2019/20 MYA Price) or (zero). To find the estimated PLC payment per base acre, multiply the Expected PLC Payment Rate by the individual farm’s PLC Yield, then multiply by the 85% payment reduction factor. A Seed Cotton Price Calculator is avaliable at the bottom of the page.

Seed Cotton Program

Beginning in 2018, seed cotton became an eligible commodity for PLC and ARC-CO payments. Seed cotton is unginned upland cotton- a combination of both cotton lint and cotton seed. The reference price for seed cotton is 36.7 cents per lb and is fixed by legislation. The marketing year average (MYA) price for seed cotton is an average price of cotton lint and cotton seed weighted by production. You an use the tool below to see how we calculated our seed cotton MYA price estimate or adjust values to see how the price would change. The default values are the values used in the current month’s cottonseed price estimate. Cotton lint prices and production estimates come from the USDA WASDE report and cottonseed prices and production estimates come from the USDA ERS Oil Crops Outlook report.
